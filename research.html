About me

In Madison, I am a member of the Graham Group, advised by Mike Graham. I work at the intersection of fluid mechanics, nonlinear dynamics, and machine learning to better understand and control turbulence. I am using ideas from nonlinear dynamics and machine learning to form a dynamical theory of turbulence, which can be leveraged to mitigate the undesired properties of turbulence.

At Princeton, I was advised by Clancy Rowley and Lex Smits. There, I researched the swimming and flight of animals using theory, experiments, and computations. I solved a 30-year-old mystery of why many swimming and flying animals flap their fins and wings the same way. For my work, I was named the Porter Ogden Jacobus Fellow.
